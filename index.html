Box Cricket

  1. Each team will consist of 10+3 players.
  2. Every over will be bowled by fresh bowler.
  3. Bowling rules:
    • Bowlers can bowl moving within the box.
    • Side arm is not allowed.
    • Second over will be power play over. The runs scored in this over will be doubled.
    • Run out or stumping is out, ball will be counted and one run will be added to the score.
    • For box, out-ball will be counted, wicket will be counted and one run will be added to the score.
  4. No ball rules:
    • Short ball will be considered as no ball.
    • Full toss above knee height will be considered as no ball (only if the batsman is on the crease).
    • Full toss ball behind the stumps will be considered as no ball.
    • 3 players can field behind 2-D line. If more than 3 players are allotted then it will be considered as no ball.
  5. Wide ball:
    • Stumping is out, ball will not be counted and one run will be added.
  6. In case of a tie:
    • First wicket will be considered.
    • If the wickets are at the same time, toss before the match started will be considered (toss win). In case of league match: Super over.
  7. The players of the batting team should be outside the field except the ones playing on the ground. No one else from the bowling team is permitted to be near the umpire except the captain.
  8. Failure to follow the above mentioned rules will result in the disqualification of the entire team.

Entry fee: ₹400

Volleyball

  1. Team members will be 6+6.
  2. Service on the line will be considered OUT, normal points on line –IN.
  3. Minimum 3 players should serve in each set.
  4. Players should compulsorily toss the ball.
  5. During service or ball toss, for foot on the line, first warning would be given by the primary referee.
  6. For net touch and line cut, direct point is given and for palm touch and roll, first warning will be given.
  7. Service should be done only after the whistle; else directly point will be given.
  8. Failure to follow the above mentioned rules will result in the disqualification of the entire team.

Entry fee: ₹300

Football

  1. There will be a total of 5 players playing in the field and 3 subs sitting outside.
  2. Rolling subs is allowed in between the game.
  3. Rise in your own half is allowed.
  4. Goal keeper cannot touch the ball with hands. In case he touches the ball, there will be penalty. He can come out of the D and play as a normal player also.
  5. There will be one referee standing in for a match.
  6. In case of an outside, kick start rule is applicable. Player from the opponent team should stand three yards away from the kick area.
  7. If the goalie handles the ball outsides the area, it will be considered as a foul and an indirect free kick will be awarded to the opponent team.
  8. The card system will be according to the normal FIFA rules (Two consecutive yellow cards will result into a red card and will lead to match suspension. The player will also miss the next match. If a player is booked with yellow card and he is booked again in another match, it will result in his suspension in the next match).
  9. In case of a free kick, the opponent team players should be standing at least three yards away from the spot kick region.
  10. In case of a draw, penalties will decide the outcome of the game (three penalties) followed by sudden death. The penalty should enter the goal in just one drop or director.
  11. During penalties, the first bounce of kick should mandatorily be after half line of court.
  12. Failure to follow the above mentioned rules will result in the disqualification of the entire team.

Entry fee: ₹300

Kho-Kho

  1. 12 people should be in team (9+3).
  2. The following will be considered as fouls:
    • Action of touching chaser by hand after uttering the word ‘Kho’.
    • Turning of shoulder line more than a right angle to the direction an attacker is going.
    • Attacker recedes receding is decide on the rear foot.
    • An attacker gives ‘Kho’ by touching the extended arm or leg of chaser.
    • An attacker touches central lane.
    • An attackers start chase with foot outside the cross lane on one side but chases further on the other side.
    • Shoulder-line of an attacker indicates the direction but it changed later on.
    • Ground of the central lane or other half is touched by any part of the body of an attacker.
    • Leaving one’s square without getting or before getting a Kho.
    • If chaser losses contact of a cross lane in the process of giving Kho i.e. goes beyond the cross lane.
  3. If fall occurs, fall in the lobby [0] will be considered.
  4. If attacker goes outside the ground border, s/he will be considered as out.
  5. After touching the poles, player announcing ‘KHO’ is mandatory.
  6. After the last player of a batch is out, the next batch should be in ground before the next Kho.
  7. To recover fall, back Kho is essential.
  8. In case of a tie, three minutes game will be provided to both the sides.
  9. Failure to follow the above mentioned rules will result in the disqualification of the entire team.

Entry fee: ₹250

Badminton

  1. The game starts with a coin toss. Whoever wins the toss gets to decide whether they would serve or receive first OR what side of the court they want to be on. The side losing the toss shall then exercise the remaining choice.
  2. At no time during the game should the player touch the net with either his/her racquet or his/her body.
  3. The shuttlecock should not be carried on or come to rest on the racquet.
  4. A player should not reach over the net to hit the shuttlecock.
  5. During the serve, a player should not touch any of the lines of court, until the server strikes the shuttlecock. The shuttlecock should always be hit from below the waist.
  6. A point is added to the player’s score at the end when s/he wins a rally.
  7. Service starts from even quadrant of court; odd point-left side, even point-right side.
  8. Double hit is counted as a foul.
  9. After a point win, the winning team should rotate before the next serve.
  10. Deuce condition will be settled by advantage method (Advantage method must be used only thrice in one match. After that, deuce will be settled by sudden death).
  11. Service should be done only after the whistle; else direct point will be given.
  12. The decision to change the shuttlecock shall be taken by the referee only if necessary.
  13. If a player’s racquet hits the ground during a rally, it will be counted as a foul and point will be given to the opponent.
  14. If partners’ racquets clash during a rally, it will be counted as a foul.
  15. Failure to follow the above mentioned rules will result in the disqualification of the entire team.

Entry fee: ₹60 (Doubles/Mixed)

Table Tennis

  1. The choice of playing position at the table and order of service is determined by the toss of a coin at the start of the match.
  2. The number of sets of a game depends on the number of participants.
  3. The players should exchange ends after each set, if the game consists of more than one set.
  4. The ball should be struck so that it touches the server’s court first, passes directly over or around the net and then touches the receiver’s court. At the instant of contact of the racquet on the ball in service, both the handle and the ball must be behind the end line of the server’s court.
  5. If the ball hits the ceiling, it will be counted as a fault.
  6. Failure to follow the above mentioned rules will result in the disqualification of the entire team.

Entry fee: ₹40(Singles) | ₹60 (Doubles/Mixed)
